About EBI-BD

Bridging the gap between scientific evidence and clinical practice.

Bipolar disorder affects approximately 39.5 million people worldwide. Nearly half of individuals with BD do not adhere to prescribed treatments, often due to perceived inefficacy, poor tolerability, or insufficient involvement in clinical decision-making.

EBI-BD is the first living umbrella review to consolidate all available network and pairwise meta-analytic evidence on the efficacy and safety of treatments for bipolar disorder. Built within the U-REACH framework, EBI-BD assesses the certainty of evidence using the GRADE framework and makes all results freely accessible through an interactive digital platform.

The platform is designed to support shared decision-making between clinicians and individuals with lived experience of BD, providing transparent, up-to-date evidence to inform personalized treatment choices.

Scope of the Review

Comprehensive coverage across phases, age groups, and interventions.

All Mood Phases

Bipolar depression, mania/mixed episodes, and maintenance phase - covering the full illness trajectory.

All Age Groups

Evidence from adults, children and adolescents, and mixed-age populations to support lifespan care.

Platform Features

Interactive tools designed for clinicians, researchers, and guideline developers.

Interactive Database

Filter and explore the full dataset by age group, BD phase, intervention, control, outcome, and certainty of evidence. Visualize distributions and download data freely.

Preference-Based Tool

Select which adverse events matter most and assign personalized weights. The TOPSIS algorithm generates individualized medication rankings based on your priorities.

Living Review

Updated at least every two years, with additional updates triggered by major changes in the literature, ensuring the evidence stays current.

Open Access

All data, results, and tools are freely available. The platform was co-designed with clinicians, guideline makers, and people with lived experience of BD.
